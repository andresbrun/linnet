# Linnet Positioning

## Core Idea

Linnet is a calm personal assistant for relationships.

It helps people stay close to the ones they love by remembering birthdays, anniversaries, check-ins, traditions, and the recurring moments that deserve thoughtful follow-through.

## The Problem

People care deeply, but real life gets busy.

Important dates are forgotten.
Follow-ups happen too late.
Good intentions stay in their head instead of turning into actions.

Most existing solutions split this problem in the wrong way:

- birthday apps focus on dates, cards, and countdowns
- personal CRMs feel too manual and too clinical
- couples apps are too narrow
- calendar apps manage logistics, not closeness

## The Space Linnet Owns

Linnet sits between a birthday reminder app and a personal CRM.

It is not trying to manage work contacts.
It is not trying to become a social network.
It is not trying to be a gamified couples app.

It is a private, low-friction, consumer tool for staying present in the relationships that matter most.

## Who It Is For

- Busy adults who care about friends, family, and partners but struggle with follow-through
- People with long-distance relationships or distributed families
- Users who want a softer, more personal alternative to productivity apps
- People who want help remembering the next thoughtful action, not just the next date

## Who It Is Not For

- Sales or networking users who want a professional CRM
- Couples looking for quizzes, games, or therapy-style prompts only
- Users who only want gift cards, shopping hooks, or a birthday countdown

## Product Promise

Linnet helps you remember the people you love, the moments that matter, and the next thoughtful thing to do.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. People, not just dates

Linnet keeps context around every reminder, so moments stay tied to real relationships.

### 2. Thoughtful follow-through

It is not enough to know a date is coming.
Linnet helps users act before it slips by.

### 3. Calm by design

The experience should feel warm, private, and uncluttered.
No heavy CRM feel. No social feed. No noise.

### 4. Private by default

The app should feel trustworthy.
Personal relationships are intimate, and the product should treat them that way.

## Short Positioning Lines

- A calm personal assistant for relationships
- Stay close without keeping it all in your head
- Remember people better, not just dates
- Thoughtful follow-through for the people you love

## Launch Angle

The strongest launch framing is:

"Linnet helps you stay close to the people you love with birthdays, anniversaries, check-ins, and thoughtful follow-through."

This keeps the app discoverable through familiar reminder language while differentiating it from simple countdown and birthday utilities.
